# 02 — Positioning Strategy

*Phase 1 deliverable. How Devora positions against barq — and wins on a different axis.*

## 1. The one-line positioning

> **devora — dev + aura.** One senior team that designs, builds and launches websites end to end, in English and Arabic, with a presence you can feel.

Where barq competes on **price and breadth** for local SMBs, Devora competes on **craft, ownership and outcomes** for teams whose website is a serious business asset.

## 2. Devora vs barq

| Axis | barq | devora |
|---|---|---|
| Buyer | Israeli SMB, price-sensitive | Founders/teams who live by their site |
| Language | Hebrew | **Bilingual EN/AR (RTL native)** |
| Promise | Cheap, fast, subscription | **One team, end to end; craft + engineering** |
| Team | Vendor with many hands | **Small, senior, accountable** |
| Proof | Round-number stats | **Real live sites, honest narrative** |
| AI | Feature list | **Engineering-led automation** |
| Differentiator | "SMB + monthly" | **"dev + aura" — the trade-off refused** |

## 3. Ideal customer profile (ICP)

**Primary:** founders, marketers and studio owners (MENA + worldwide, remote-friendly) who:
- need a site that *performs*, not just looks good;
- value working with the makers directly, not a rotating cast;
- often need **bilingual EN/AR** with correct RTL;
- want one accountable team across strategy, brand, design, build and growth;
- will pay a premium for craft and speed, and value an ongoing partner after launch.

**Best-fit verticals:** studios & creative, e-commerce & retail, professional services (clinics/firms), hospitality & venues, startups & SaaS, local & regional bilingual brands.

## 4. Differentiation — the three wedges

1. **One team, end to end.** The people who plan it build it. No middlemen, no lost handoffs. (Directly answers barq's vendor-y, multi-hand feel.)
2. **dev + aura.** Engineering rigor *and* the presence great design leaves behind — we refuse the "pretty template vs. lifeless code" trade-off.
3. **Bilingual by design.** Native EN & AR with real RTL — a structural advantage barq (Hebrew-only) can't match for the MENA market.

## 5. Messaging pillars

| Pillar | Says | Proof points |
|---|---|---|
| **End to end** | Strategy → brand → design → code → launch → growth, one team | 5 disciplines, 5-step process, engagement models |
| **Craft you can feel** | Editorial, fast, considered | Design system, motion, real live sites |
| **Bilingual & regional** | EN/AR, RTL done right, MENA + worldwide | Whole site in both languages |
| **Beyond launch** | We don't disappear — a Care Plan keeps it growing | Care Plan, support windows, AI/automation |
| **Own the result** | Clean, documented, editable — no lock-in | CMS + training, code handover |

## 6. Brand voice

- **Editorial, confident, minimal.** Short sentences. Concrete nouns. No hype, no filler agency-speak ("synergy," "solutions provider," "cutting-edge").
- **Outcome-first.** Lead with what the client gets, not what we do.
- **Warm, not corporate.** "We stick around." "No pitch, just your project."
- **Honest.** No invented metrics or testimonials; "placeholder figures — every project gets a custom quote."
- **Bilingual register:** English is crisp and modern; Arabic is elegant MSA (فصحى), brand names and numerals kept LTR, tracking neutralized for ligatures.

## 7. Proof-of-difference (kept honest)

Because we chose **not** to fabricate stats or testimonials, credibility is built from:
- Two **real, live** client sites (zawiya.studio, aldarb.co) with honest challenge/approach/outcome narratives.
- A transparent **Process** and **Pricing** page (nothing hidden).
- A clear **ownership** promise (you keep the code and CMS).
- Optional, clearly-flagged slots for future real proof (testimonials, logos, stats) once available.
